    Crime Data

    Data was recorded for the city of Los Angeles

    Check out the rest of this page to explore the stats regarding crimes in Los Angeles. Learn about which crimes are most frequent, when they happen the most, and which locations you should maybe avoid.

    Frequency of crimes per day

    The frequency data shows a clear seasonal trend in crime occurances, with the highest number of incidents occurring on August 1st and overall peaks during the summer months. This aligns with periods of increased outdoor activity, tourism, and idle time, especially among youth who are out of school. Interestingly, crime rates begin to decline sharply in early September. This timing coincides with the start of the academic school year, suggesting that structured routines, school schedules, and fewer people on the streets contribute to a reduction in criminal activity. The pattern highlights how social rhythms and public behavior like vacation periods and holidays can significantly influence urban crime dynamics. Understanding these seasonal shifts is crucial for resource planning and preventative strategies during high-risk periods.

    Top 10 Crime Types

    The data reveals that vehicle-related crimes dominate the top 10 most frequent offenses in Los Angeles. At the top of the list is Stolen Vehicle, accounting for about 15% of all reported incidents. This is followed by crimes like "Burglary from Vehicle" and "Petty Theft from Motor Vehicle", underscoring how cars are a primary target for criminal activity. These patterns suggest that residents should exercise caution about what they leave visible inside their vehicles and be strategic about where they park, especially in high-risk areas. Other common crimes, such as Simple Assault, Felony Vandalism, and Robbery, point to both property-related and interpersonal threats. Lastly, Simple Assault from an Intimate Partner also ranks among the most frequent crimes. This highlights the ongoing issue of domestic violence that is seen across the world. Overall, the list of crimes reflects a complex urban environment where spaces are vulnerable, regardless of if they are public or private. Prevention strategies must address a wide range of threats.

    Map of Crime Locations

    This interactive map plots the geographic locations of reported crimes in LA, using latitude and longitude data to mark each incident. This visualization provides a spatial understanding of crime distribution throughout the city. By zooming in and out, you can identify specific neighborhoods or intersections with high concentrations of crime, revealing potential hotspots that might otherwise go unnoticed in aggregated data. This kind of mapping is especially useful for urban planners, local law enforcement, and community organizations seeking to allocate resources more effectively. It also empowers residents to become more aware of safety concerns in their immediate surroundings, encouraging informed decision-making about mobility and local engagement.
